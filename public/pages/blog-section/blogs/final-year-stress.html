Why Is Final Year So Stressful?

Your final year is packed with important tasks, all of which seem like they could define your future. Deadlines are tighter, expectations are higher, and there's a constant need to prove yourself – to your lecturers, potential employers, and even your peers. On top of that, there’s the pressure to secure a job post-graduation, and every time someone asks you "What are your plans after uni?", the panic sets in.


This pressure can be overwhelming and cause students to overwork themselves, sacrificing sleep, social time, and even self-care. However, burning out isn’t going to help you smash those final assignments, nor is it going to land you your dream job. So, how do you balance it all? It starts with prioritising, planning, and knowing when to take a break.


The Importance of Taking Breaks

Before we get into balancing uni work and social life, let's address something crucial: taking breaks is not wasting time. In fact, breaks are essential to avoiding burnout, staying focused, and managing stress. It’s easy to fall into the trap of working non-stop, thinking you’ll be more productive – but research shows that taking regular breaks actually boosts your concentration and creativity.


This is where digital card games like Overexposed come in handy. Instead of zoning out in front of Netflix during a break, engage in something fun and interactive that can recharge your energy. A quick round of Overexposed is a great way to take your mind off your work while staying connected with your mates.


How to Balance Your Uni Work and Social Life

So, how exactly do you keep up with your final year responsibilities without completely losing your social life? It’s all about finding balance and being intentional with your time. Here are some practical tips to help you manage your final year stress while still enjoying yourself.


1. Make Study Breaks Social

One of the best ways to relieve stress is by connecting with your friends. However, with busy schedules, it can be hard to find time to hang out without falling behind on work. Instead of sacrificing your social life or your studies, why not combine the two?


• Set regular study breaks with your friends. After an hour or two of focused work, take a break to play a quick game.


• Digital card games are ideal for taking short breaks. They allow you to have fun, laugh, and unwind with your mates, even if you're all stuck in different corners of the library or working from home.


• Whether it's a round of "Never Have I Ever" or calling out your mates in "Most Likely To", these games can provide the perfect mental reset before getting back into your assignments.


The point is spending your breaks in a way that’s not draining. Set a timer, play a few rounds, then get back to work. That way, you're not only taking care of your mental health, but you're also boosting your productivity.

2. Plan Pre-Drinks That Help You Unwind

After a long week of juggling coursework and job applications, you're going to want to blow off some steam. But instead of mindlessly scrolling through TikTok or drowning in pre-drink playlists, why not add a bit more fun to the mix?


• Use your pre-drinks as a chance to chill with your mates by playing your favourite digital card game.


• Turning pre-drinks into game night is a brilliant way to bond with your friends, especially if you're all feeling the stress of final year. Plus, it keeps the atmosphere light and fun before heading out.


• These games add excitement to your hangouts, with options like "Truth or Dare" or custom challenges that will have everyone laughing and connecting on a deeper level.


By adding games to your pre-drinks routine, you're not only having fun, but you're also giving yourself a chance to relax in a social setting before hitting the town. This helps keep your social life intact without feeling like you're wasting time.


3. Stay Organised to Make Time for Fun

One of the biggest stressors in final year is feeling like there’s too much to do and not enough time. The key to managing this is getting organised. By creating a solid plan for your time, you'll be able to stay on top of your workload without sacrificing your social life.


• Make a weekly schedule that includes time for studying, applying for jobs, and socialising. Prioritise your deadlines, but don’t forget to pencil in some time to unwind.


• Use a planner or a time-management app to break down your tasks into smaller, more manageable chunks. This way, you can avoid the last-minute cramming sessions that make you feel like you’re drowning in work.


• Plan your social hangouts in advance, too. Knowing that you have a night out or a game night planned can motivate you to finish your tasks on time.


Through staying organised, you'll find it easier to balance your work and social life. And remember, it's perfectly okay to schedule in time for fun. Just like your study sessions, fun time is essential for your mental well-being.
